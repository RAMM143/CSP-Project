Detected disease is Mango Cutting Weevil

Mango Cutting Weevil

Identification:

  • The mango leaf-cutting weevil is a light tan beetle with black wing cases. It's a pest of mango trees in tropical Asia.
  • The main damage is caused by the female weevils, which cut young leaves. Infested shoots become almost leafless. When the density of weevils is high, the adults can cause serious damage by their feeding alone.

Sample Images:


Description:

The most obvious symptom of attack by D. marginatus are young leaves that are cut and have fallen to the ground. Stripped shoots can be easily seen on the infested plant. Adult feeding produces 'windowpanes' on young leaves.


Preventive Measures


Collect and destroy eggs: Collect leaves with eggs, dry them, and burn them.
Plow the soil: Plow the soil under the host plant's crown to kill pupae.
Apply insecticide: Apply insecticide when young leaves are 3 cm wide. Insecticides such as trichlorphon, deltamethrin, and fenvalerate are effective.
Spray chemicals: Spray chemicals such as Monocrotophos (0.04%) or Dimethoate (0.06%) to control scale insects.
Remove webs: Use a "leaf web" removing device to remove webs made by leaf webbers.
