Detected disease is Mango Anthracnose

Mango Anthracnose

Identification:

  • Leaves: Small, angular, brown to black spots that can enlarge and form dead areas. The spots may fall out of the leaves during dry weather.
  • Fruits: Small, light-colored spots on the skin. The fungus remains dormant in green fruit during the growing season. Under favorable conditions, small dark spots form and rapidly enlarge. Pink spore masses grow on the infected tissue.
  • Flowers: Flower blight, which can result in low yield and shoot dieback.

Sample Images:


Description:

Mango anthracnose is a fungal infection that affects mango plants before and after harvest. It is caused by the fungus Colletotrichum gloeosporioides var minor. The disease is most destructive in countries with high humidity during the growing season.


Preventive Measures


Wider plant spacing can prevent severe epidemics.
Interplanting mango trees with other types of trees that are not hosts of mango anthracnose can prevent epidemics.
Copper fungicides can be used to treat mango anthracnose.
Sprays should be applied regularly from when flowers begin to form.
Hot water treatment of 55 and 60°C can be effective against post-harvest anthracnose of mango.
Prune trees yearly and remove fallen plant debris from the ground.
Give plants plenty of room and good air circulation, and avoid overhead watering to limit wet leaves and fruit.
During harvest, remove fruit by clipping stems rather than tearing fruit off. Remove infected material after harvest and destroy it to reduce disease risk for the following year.

Anthracnose spreads through water, so avoid overhead watering and prune trees when the canopy is dry.
Collect and dispose of infected material
Collect and dispose of fallen infested fruits and diseased leaves, flowers, twigs, and fruits.
Prune dying branches
Prune dying branches by cutting the branch back to below the edge of the dead or symptomatic areas. Disinfect the pruning tool after each plant.
Avoid areas with consistent rain
If possible, avoid areas that consistently have rain during the flowering season.
Use sulfur dusts and sprays
Sulfur dusts and sprays have been demonstrated to be very effective and are exempt from tolerance.
