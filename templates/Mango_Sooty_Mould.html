Detected disease is Mango Sooty Mould

Mango Sooty Mould

Identification:

  • eaf coating: A black, velvety coating on the leaves. In severe cases, the entire tree can turn black.
  • Dark spots: Small dark spots that grow and spread, eventually affecting the entire panicle.
  • Infected parts: Infected flower parts and young fruits fall off the inflorescence.
  • Stunting: Plants may show stunted growth.

Sample Images:


Description:

The disease is common in the orchards where mealy bug, scale insect and hopper are not controlled efficiently. The disease in the field is recognized by the presence of a black velvety coating, i.e., sooty mould on the leaf surface.


Preventive Measures


Controlling sap-feeding insects and ants that protect them
Starting insect control as soon as sooty mold is observed
Building physical barriers around trees to prevent ants and sap-sucking insects
Fertilizing and watering trees to ensure natural resistance to parasites
Spraying with a solution of Phosphamidon 40 SL and Maida
Spraying with a strong stream of water and mild detergent
Spraying with Liquid Copper Fungicide
Spraying with alternating sprays of Wettable sulphur, Tridemorph, and Bavistin



CULTURAL CONTROL

CHEMICAL CONTROL
Use neem oil to repel insects and reduce the growth of the fungus. You can also spray plants with insecticidal soap or dish soap. Spray trees with Copper Oxychloride (0.3%). You can also use a horticultural oil to control the insects that produce honeydew.

Prune diseased twigs 2-3 inches below the affected area.
Spray with Liquid Copper Fungicide.
Spray with a strong stream of water and mild detergent.
Spray with PlcoCure natural fungicide.
