Detected disease is Mango Gall Midge

Mango Gall Midge

Identification:

  • Adults are small flies, about 1-2 mm long, with the males being slightly smaller.
  • They have a wing length of 1.0-1.5 mm.
  • Eye facets are circular, but further apart laterally than in other genera and the tarsal claws are toothed.
  • Both sexes have different antennae size, with males having the longest. The males have distal claspers on its abdomen.

Sample Images:


Description:

The mango gall midge is a small fly that can be found in most mango-growing areas. The larvae of the mango gall midge feed on the plant tissue, causing abnormal growth called galls. These galls can damage the leaves, flowers, fruit, and shoots of the mango tree.


Preventive Measures



Cultivate resistant varieties
Monitor fields regularly
Hand-pick the insects
Remove debris and broken branches
Remove weeds regularly
Collect and destroy surviving stages of midges in fallen leaves
Hoe under the tree canopy, then irrigate to kill pupating midges in the soil
Properly prune after fruit harvesting
Burn damaged branches away from the orchard
Source plant material from clean, accredited suppliers
Check the orchard frequently for new pests and unusual symptoms


CULTURAL CONTROL

CHEMICAL CONTROL
Planting resistant mango varieties KINGCODE ELITE® 50EC (10 ml in 20 Litres water)
Maintenance of field hygiene/ sanitation LEXUS® 247SC (8 ml in 20 Litres of water)
Proper weed control (weeds are alternate hosts of the pests) TOMARADO® 500SC (10 ml in 20 Litres water)
Pruning the infested branches
Mixed and intercropping farming reduces the pest population
Ploughing of the orchards exposes pupating and diapausing larvae to sun heat which kills them
Avoid movement of infested plants to new areas

Inspect mango trees for misshapen leaves and increased leaf fall. Examine the leaves for the presence of blister-shaped galls and shot-holes. Inspect stems, fruit and flowers for similar symptoms.
