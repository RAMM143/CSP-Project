Healthy Cassava

Healthy Cassava

Identification:

A healthy cassava plant typically exhibits the following characteristics:
  • 1)Sturdy and upright growth: A healthy cassava plant has a strong stem that supports the leaves and the weight of the plant. It stands upright without leaning or bending excessively.
  • 2)Vibrant green foliage: The leaves of a healthy cassava plant are generally lush, with a vibrant green color. They should be well-developed, showing no signs of wilting, yellowing, or necrosis (browning).
  • 3)Well-formed and unblemished leaves: The leaves should be smooth and have a consistent shape and size. They should not show any signs of deformities, spots, or lesions caused by diseases or pests.
  • 4)Adequate leaf coverage: A healthy cassava plant typically has a dense canopy of leaves, with sufficient leaf area to capture sunlight for photosynthesis. This ensures good plant growth and productivity.
  • 5)Active growth and development: A healthy cassava plant shows active growth with new leaves emerging regularly. It exhibits continuous development and the formation of new nodes and internodes along the stem.
  • 6)Vigorous root system: The roots of a healthy cassava plant should be well-developed and firm. They should be free from rot, soft spots, or discoloration. The fibrous roots extending from the main storage roots should be white or creamy in color.
  • 7)Normal progression of plant stages: A healthy cassava plant follows a typical growth pattern, progressing from stem cuttings or seeds to vegetative growth, flowering, and eventually the formation of storage roots. It should exhibit appropriate timing and progression through these stages.
